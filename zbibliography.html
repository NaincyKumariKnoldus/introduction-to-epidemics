Annotated bibliography¶

Alt14

Christian Althous. Estimating the reproduction number of Zaire ebolavirus (EBOV) during the 2014 outbreak in West Africa. PLOS Currents Outbreaks, September 2014. URL: http://dx.doi.org/10.1371/currents.outbreaks.91afb5e0f279e7f29e7056095255b288.

ER59

Paul Erdos and Alfred Renyi. On random graphs. Publicationes Mathematicæ, 6:290–297, 1959. The paper that revolutionised the field of graph theory and paved to way for the modern approach to network science. It contains proofs of a wide range of properties of random – or as they are now called, Erdos-Renyi or ER – graphs, which serve as the benchmark against which the behaviours of other topologies are measured.

Het00

Herbert Hethcote. The mathematics of infectious diseases. SIAM Review, 42(4):599–653, December 2000. The best readily-available introduction to the mathematics of the classical epidemic models. URL: http://dx.doi.org/10.1137/S0036144500371907.

Hof98

Paul Hoffman. The man who loved only numbers: The story of Paul Erdos and the search for mathematical truth. Hyperion, 1998. ISBN 978-0786863624.

KW91

Jeffrey Kephart and Steve White. Directed-graph epidemiological models of computer viruses. In Proceedings of Research in Security and Privacy, 343–359. IEEE Press, May 1991. An application of network-based epidemiology to computer viruses. The mathematics of spread are essentially identical, and even the methods of infection – through contacts between ‘infected’ and ‘susceptible’ computers – are sufficiently analogous to make the two systems behave very similarly. The main differences come because computer viruses, unlike ‘natural’ diseases, are targetted at specific hosts. This can be a lot more effective (in the short term, at least) than random evolution. URL: http://dx.doi.org/10.1109/RISP.1991.130801.

Rov17

Carlo Rovelli. Reality is not what it seems: The journey to quantum gravity. Penguin, 2017. ISBN 978-0141983219.

VTM07

Emilia Vynnycky, Amy Trindall, and Punam Mangtani. Estimates of the reproduction numbers of Spanish influenza using morbidity data. International Journal of Epidemiology, 36:881–889, 2007. URL: http://dx.doi.org/10.1093/ije/dym071.
